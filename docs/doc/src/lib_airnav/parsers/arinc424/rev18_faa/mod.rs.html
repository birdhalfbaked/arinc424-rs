2//! This module contains the parser for the ARINC 424 Revision 18 standard.
3//!
4//! More context is that the FAA applies the following exceptions to the standard:
5//!
6//! # CIFP Record Types and Coding Notes
7//!
8//! This document summarizes the record types and coding conventions used in the **Coded Instrument Flight Procedures (CIFP)** dataset.
9//!
10//! ---
11//!
12//! # Record Types
13//!
14//! The CIFP currently provides the following record types:
15//!
16//! | Record Type | Code(s) |
17//! |-------------|---------|
18//! | Airports | PA |
19//! | Heliports | HA |
20//! | Runways | PG |
21//! | VHF Navaids | D |
22//! | NDB Navaids | DB |
23//! | Terminal Navaids | PN |
24//! | Localizer and Glide Slope | PI |
25//! | Path Points (Primary & Continuation) | PP |
26//! | Minimum Safe Altitude (MSA) | PS, HS |
27//! | Enroute Waypoints | EA |
28//! | Terminal Waypoints | PC, HC |
29//! | Standard Instrument Departures (SIDs) | PD |
30//! | Standard Terminal Arrival Routes (STARs) | PE |
31//! | Approaches (including Level of Service continuation) | PF, HF |
32//! | Airways | ER |
33//! | Class B/C/D Airspace | UC |
34//! | Special Use Airspace (Primary & Continuation) | UR |
35//! | Grid MORA | AS |
36//!
37//! ---
38//!
39//! # Airports and Heliports
40//!
41//! - The published **ICAO Airport Identifier** is used whenever available.
42//! - If no ICAO identifier exists, the published FAA Airport Identifier is used.
43//! - The **IATA Code** field contains the FAA Airport Identifier.
44//! - If the airport identifier is four characters long, the IATA field is left blank.
45//! - The **Longest Runway (5.54)** field does **not always represent the longest hard-surface runway** at the airport.
46//!
47//! ---
48//!
49//! # Runways
50//!
51//! - Runway suffixes include:
52//!   - `W` — Water
53//!   - `S` — Soft surface
54//!   - `G` — Glider
55//!   - `U` — Ultralight
56//!   - Numeric suffixes — Assault strips
57//! - Non-numeric runway identifiers are included without the `RW` prefix.
58//! - Runway Gradient (5.212) and Ellipsoid Height (5.225) are included when available.
59//! - If no magnetic variation is available, Runway Magnetic Bearings (5.58) are calculated using the **World Magnetic Model (WMM)**.
60//!
61//! ---
62//!
63//! # Waypoints and Fixes
64//!
65//! ## Supported Domestic Fix Types
66//!
67//! - Reporting Point
68//! - Waypoint
69//! - RNAV Waypoint
70//! - VFR Waypoint
71//! - Computer Navigation
72//! - Terminal Waypoint
73//! - ATC Coordination
74//! - GPS Waypoint
75//! - Military Reporting Point
76//! - Military Waypoint
77//! - NRS Waypoint
78//! - Radar
79//!
80//! ### General Rules
81//!
82//! - Waypoints with **all-numeric identifiers are not included**.
83//! - Offshore fixes may use:
84//!   - Customer/Area Code: `USA`
85//!   - ICAO Code: `K ` or `P ` (character followed by blank)
86//!
87//! ### Waypoint Type (5.42)
88//!
89//! | Value | Meaning |
90//! |--------|---------|
91//! | R | Ground-based |
92//! | W | Satellite-based |
93//! | C | Both ground and satellite |
94//!
95//! Radar-only fixes are coded as `R`.
96//!
97//! ### Waypoint Name Format Indicator
98//!
99//! - Field 5.196 is not populated.
100//!
101//! ### PC Records
102//!
103//! PC records may be used for named terminal waypoints when:
104//!
105//! - Used at only one airport
106//! - Not used on an enroute airway
107//!
108//! Otherwise EA records are used.
109//!
110//! Some unnamed terminal waypoints also use PC records.
111//!
112//! PC waypoints:
113//!
114//! - Carry the Customer/Area Code of the parent airport.
115//! - Retain their own ICAO Code even when different from the parent airport.
116//!
117//! ---
118//!
119//! # Navaids
120//!
121//! ## PN vs DB
122//!
123//! A PN record may be used for an NDB when:
124//!
125//! - Used at only one airport
126//! - Not used on an enroute airway
127//! - Assigned a five-letter name
128//!
129//! Otherwise a DB record is used.
130//!
131//! ## Additional Rules
132//!
133//! - DME-only facilities have magnetic variation computed using WMM.
134//! - If a VOR frequency is unavailable:
135//!   - VOR Frequency field = `00000`
136//! - Other unavailable frequencies are left blank.
137//!
138//! ### Navaid Class 3 (5.35)
139//!
140//! | Code | Meaning |
141//! |------|---------|
142//! | H | High |
143//! | L | Low |
144//! | T | Terminal |
145//! | U | Undetermined |
146//!
147//! ### Navaid Class 5
148//!
149//! `N` indicates a VORTAC when VOR and TACAN coordinates differ by **0.1 NM or more**.
150//!
151//! ### DME Elevation
152//!
153//! If unavailable separately, the associated VOR elevation is used.
154//!
155//! ### Figure of Merit (5.149)
156//!
157//! - Derived from Navaid Class.
158//! - If the Navaid Class is unknown, Figure of Merit = `3`.
159//!
160//! ### Weather Capability
161//!
162//! HIWAS and TWEB are coded as:
163//!
164//! `A`
165//!
166//! ---
167//!
168//! # Airways
169//!
170//! U.S. airways include:
171//!
172//! - Enroute Airways
173//! - Area Navigation Routes
174//! - RNAV IFR Terminal Transition Routes
175//! - ATS Routes
176//!
177//! Canadian and other non-U.S. airways are **not included**.
178//!
179//! ## Route Type (5.7)
180//!
181//! | Code | Meaning |
182//! |------|---------|
183//! | O | Conventional |
184//! | R | RNAV |
185//!
186//! The Minimum Altitude field contains:
187//!
188//! - Conventional MEA for conventional routes.
189//! - GNSS MEA for RNAV routes.
190//! - Conventional MEA if no GNSS MEA exists.
191//!
192//! ### Route Level (5.19)
193//!
194//! | Prefix | Level |
195//! |---------|-------|
196//! | V, T | L |
197//! | J, Q | H |
198//! | Others | Blank |
199//!
200//! ATS Routes do not contain directional restrictions (5.115).
201//!
202//! ---
203//!
204//! # Approaches
205//!
206//! Supported procedures include:
207//!
208//! - ILS
209//! - LOC (Category I only)
210//! - VOR
211//! - NDB
212//! - GPS Overlay
213//! - GPS
214//! - RNAV (GPS)
215//! - RNAV (RNP)
216//! - Helicopter GPS/RNAV approaches
217//!
218//! PI records are only included when the corresponding procedure exists in the CIFP.
219//!
220//! ## LDA
221//!
222//! LDA procedures with both:
223//!
224//! - LDA minima
225//! - Glide Slope minima
226//!
227//! are coded only to the **LDA minima**.
228//!
229//! ## ARINC 424 Version 19
230//!
231//! Version 19 applies to:
232//!
233//! - SID/STAR/Approach Identifier
234//! - Route Type
235//! - Route Qualifier 1
236//! - Level of Service continuation records
237//! - Circling procedure altitude coding
238//!
239//! For RNAV (RNP):
240//!
241//! - Column 20 = `H`
242//! - Column 119 = `F`
243//! - Final and missed approach route identifiers begin with `H`.
244//!
245//! RF legs are coded as fly-by unless followed by an Hx leg.
246//!
247//! ### Holding
248//!
249//! Waypoint Description Code `H` is not used for:
250//!
251//! - Arrivals
252//! - SIDs
253//! - STAR holding
254//!
255//! Holding records (EP) are unavailable.
256//!
257//! ---
258//!
259//! # Controlled Airspace
260//!
261//! - Airspace names come from the legal description.
262//! - Airspace Center uses the ICAO identifier of the primary airport.
263//! - Unit Indicator for altitudes described as **GND** is:
264//!
265//! `A` (AGL)
266//!
267//! ---
268//!
269//! # Special Use Airspace
270//!
271//! ## Time Code
272//!
273//! | Value | Meaning |
274//! |--------|---------|
275//! | C | Continuous |
276//! | Blank | Part-time |
277//!
278//! ### Altitudes
279//!
280//! - Altitudes are coded only on the first record.
281//! - Upper limits are inclusive ("to and including").
282//! - Ground-based limits use Unit Indicator `A`.
283//!
284//! ### Geometry
285//!
286//! Arc endpoints creating gaps greater than **0.02 NM** may be recalculated to better match published radii.
287//!
288//! ### Totally Excluded Volumes
289//!
290//! Lower and upper limits are coded:
291//!
292//! ```
293//! GND A0000A
294//! ```
295//!
296//! ### Continuation Records
297//!
298//! Included only for:
299//!
300//! - Controlling Agencies (5.140)
301//!
302//! ### Special Air Traffic Rules Areas (SATR)
303//!
304//! Included as UR records where geometry permits.
305//!
306//! Restricted Airspace Type:
307//!
308//! `U`
309//!
310//! Examples include:
311//!
312//! - Anchorage SATR
313//! - Ketchikan SATR
314//! - Grand Canyon SFRA (East and West)
315//! - Luke AFB SATR
316//! - Washington DC SFRA / FRZ
317//! - Niagara Falls SATR
318//! - New York SFRA
319//! - Valparaiso SATR
320//! - Portland (VUO) SFRA
321//!
322//! National Security Areas are also coded as `U`.
323//!
324//! ### Grand Canyon SFRA
325//!
326//! The Grand Canyon SFRA is divided into:
327//!
328//! - East
329//! - West
330//!
331//! Internal sector boundaries and Flight Free Zones are included.
332//!
333//! ### Maritime Limits
334//!
335//! UR airspace based on maritime boundaries is being standardized to NOAA references while preserving legal-description coordinates.
336//!
337//! ---
338//!
339//! # Coding Information
340//!
341//! ## Waypoint Descriptor
342//!
343//! - Compulsory waypoints are **not indicated** in Waypoint Descriptor 3.
344//! - `R` indicates a course change in the final approach.
345//! - `S` is **not used**:
346//!   - Between FACF and FAF.
347//!   - Between FAF and MAP on RNAV (RNP) procedures.
348//!
349//! ---
350//!
351//! ## Final Approach Course Fix (FACF)
352//!
353//! The FACF may also be the Initial Fix (IF), allowing step-down fixes between the FACF and FAF.
354//!
355//! ---
356//!
357//! ## Minimum Safe Altitude (MSA)
358//!
359//! Normally:
360//!
361//! - MSA Center Fix is coded on the FAF.
362//!
363//! If the FAF is an RF leg:
364//!
365//! - Center Fix is coded on the FACF.
366//!
367//! If no FACF exists:
368//!
369//! - No center fix is coded.
370//!
371//! ---
372//!
373//! ## Vertical Angle
374//!
375//! The following are coded as:
376//!
377//! ```
378//! 000
379//! ```
380//!
381//! - Circling procedures
382//! - Dive-and-drive procedures
383//! - Straight-in procedures where Flight Inspection directs due to obstacles
384//!
385//! ---
386//!
387//! ## Missed Approach
388//!
389//! CA (Course-to-Altitude) legs may be used first.
390//!
391//! If no mandatory altitude exists:
392//!
393//! The coded altitude is the lowest of:
394//!
395//! - Decision Altitude (DA)
396//! - Minimum Descent Altitude (MDA)
397//! - 400 feet above airport elevation
398//!
399//! Alternate missed approaches (Route Type `Z`) are **not included**.
400//!
401//! The missed approach route type matches the final approach route.
402//!
403//! ---
404//!
405//! ## Required Navigation Performance (RNP)
406//!
407//! RNP values follow FAA Order 8260.58C:
408//!
409//! 1. HF and HM legs receive no RNP values.
410//! 2. Amendments match FAA Form 8260-3.
411//! 3. P-NOTAM coding changes use FAA Form 8260-3 values.
412//! 4. P-NOTAMs without coding changes are updated as workload permits.
413//! 5. Existing procedures are updated gradually, with preference given to updating all procedures at an airport together.
414//!
415//! ---
416//!
417//! ## Route Qualifier 2
418//!
419//! Route Qualifier 2:
420//!
421//! ```
422//! H
423//! ```
424//!
425//! is used for helicopter approaches using helipads.
426//!
427//! ---
428//!
429//! ## File Record Number (5.31)
430//!
431//! - Unique per record.
432//! - Not consecutive across the dataset.
433//! - May contain alphabetic characters or blanks.
434//!
435//! ---
436//!
437//! ## Cycle Date (5.32)
438//!
439//! Cycle dates are updated to the latest cycle for:
440//!
441//! - Newly added records
442//! - Modified records
443//! ```
